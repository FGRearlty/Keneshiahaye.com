Typical timeline: 30-45 days from offer to keys. Here's every step along the way:

1

Get Your Certificate of Eligibility (COE)

Timeline: Same day – 2 weeks

Your COE proves to lenders that you're eligible for a VA loan. You can request it online through eBenefits, through your lender, or by mail. Most lenders can pull it electronically in minutes.
 

2

Get Pre-Approved with a VA Lender

Timeline: 1 – 3 days

A VA-experienced lender will review your income, credit, and COE to tell you exactly how much home you can afford. I work with lenders who specialize in VA loans and can get this done quickly.
 

3

House Hunting with Your Agent

Timeline: 2 – 8 weeks (varies)

This is the fun part! I'll set up a custom search based on your needs, budget, and preferred neighborhoods. We'll tour homes together (or virtually if you're PCS'ing from out of state) until we find the right fit.
 

4

Make an Offer & Negotiate

Timeline: 1 – 3 days

I'll help you craft a competitive offer that includes seller concessions for closing costs when possible. VA buyers are just as competitive as any other buyer — I'll make sure sellers see that.
 

5

Home Inspection

Timeline: 1 – 2 weeks after offer accepted

A professional inspector checks the home for any issues. This is separate from the VA appraisal and gives you leverage to negotiate repairs or credits if anything comes up.
 

6

VA Appraisal

Timeline: 1 – 3 weeks

The VA sends an approved appraiser to confirm the home's value and ensure it meets Minimum Property Requirements (MPRs). This protects you from overpaying or buying a home with safety issues.
 

7

Closing Day — Get Your Keys!

Timeline: 1 – 2 hours

You'll do a final walkthrough, sign the closing documents, and receive the keys to your new home. With a VA loan and seller concessions, many of my clients bring very little (sometimes nothing) to the closing table.

Common VA Buying Pitfalls to Avoid:

Using a non-VA-savvy lender — Always work with lenders experienced in VA loans. The process is different and you need someone who knows it.

Skipping the home inspection — The VA appraisal is not a home inspection. Always get both. The inspection is your safety net.

Making large purchases before closing — Don't buy a car, furniture, or open new credit cards. Lenders re-check your credit before closing.

Not using an agent who knows VA — Some agents aren't familiar with VA-specific requirements. You need someone who can advocate for you and navigate MPR issues.
