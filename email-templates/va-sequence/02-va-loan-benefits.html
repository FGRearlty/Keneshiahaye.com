Listen, {{contact.first_name}} — as a veteran, you have access to one of the most powerful home loan programs ever created. But here's the thing — many veterans don't fully understand just how good the VA loan is compared to other options. Now, let me break this down for you.

$0

1. Zero Down Payment

While conventional loans require 5-20% down and FHA needs 3.5%, VA loans let you buy with absolutely nothing down. On a $350K home, that's up to $70,000 you keep in your savings.

NO

2. No Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI)

Conventional loans charge $150-$300/month in PMI if you put less than 20% down. FHA loans charge mortgage insurance for the entire loan term. VA loans? Zero PMI, ever. That's thousands saved annually.

%

3. Consistently Lower Interest Rates

VA loan rates are typically 0.25-0.50% lower than conventional rates. On a 30-year $300K mortgage, that difference can save you over $30,000 in total interest paid.

CAP

4. Limited Closing Costs

The VA limits what fees veterans can be charged at closing. Certain costs are outright prohibited. Plus, the seller can pay up to 4% of the purchase price toward your closing costs.

↻

5. It's a Lifetime Benefit (Reusable!)

This is the one that surprises most veterans. Your VA loan benefit isn't one-and-done. You can use it again and again — every time you PCS, upgrade, or invest. Once the previous loan is paid off (or even through a one-time restoration), your entitlement resets.
