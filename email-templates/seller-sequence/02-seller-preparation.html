Before you put a "For Sale" sign in the yard, there are a few things you can do to maximize your home's value and attract serious buyers. These steps have helped my clients sell faster and for more money — and many of them cost little to nothing.

5 Things to Do Before Listing:

1

Declutter & Depersonalize

Remove excess furniture, personal photos, and knick-knacks. Buyers need to envision themselves living in the space, not feel like guests in yours.

2

Make Minor Repairs

Fix leaky faucets, patch nail holes, replace burned-out bulbs, and touch up paint. Small issues signal bigger problems to buyers.

3

Stage Your Home

Staging doesn't have to be expensive. Simple touches like fresh flowers, neutral bedding, and clean surfaces can make a huge difference in how buyers perceive your space.

4

Invest in Professional Photography

Most buyers start their search online. Professional photos are the difference between a scroll-past and a showing request. I include this in my marketing plan at no extra cost to you.

5

Understand Your Pricing Strategy

Pricing is everything in real estate. I'll share more about this in my next email — including the biggest mistake Jacksonville sellers make and how to avoid it.

Pro Tip

Focus on curb appeal! In Florida, the exterior is the first impression buyers get. Clean the driveway, trim the landscaping, and add a fresh welcome mat. These small touches can add thousands to your sale price.
