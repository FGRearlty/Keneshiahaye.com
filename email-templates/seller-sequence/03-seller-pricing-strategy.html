Now, I understand the temptation — you want to get the most for your home, and you figure buyers will just negotiate down. But trust me, pricing too high from the start actually works against you. Let me break this down for you:

Why Overpricing Backfires:

Buyers Skip Your Listing

Today's buyers search by price range. If your home is priced above comparable properties, it simply won't show up in their search results.
 

Days on Market Climb

The longer your home sits on the market, the more buyers wonder what's wrong with it. High days-on-market signals to buyers that something is off — even if the only problem is the price.
 

You End Up Selling for Less

After multiple price reductions, homes often sell for less than they would have if priced correctly from day one. The initial excitement is gone, and buyers sense desperation.

How I Price Homes to Sell:

Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) — I analyze recent sales in your neighborhood, current listings, and market trends to determine the optimal price range.

Condition & Upgrades — I factor in your home's unique features, updates, and condition compared to similar properties.

Market Timing — Seasonality, interest rates, and local demand all play a role. I'll help you choose the right moment to list.

Strategic Positioning — I price your home to generate maximum interest and multiple showings within the first two weeks — when buyer activity is highest.

Did You Know?

Homes that are priced right from the start typically sell within 30 days in the Jacksonville market, while overpriced homes can sit for 90 days or more before needing price reductions.
