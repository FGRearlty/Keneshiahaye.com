💡 Tip of the Week

Ask for Closing Cost Credits — Sellers Are Saying Yes

Only 12% of Jacksonville homes are selling over asking price right now. That means you can negotiate. Ask your agent to request seller-paid closing cost credits or a rate buydown — in most cases, sellers would rather sweeten the deal than drop their price. On a $300K home, this can save you $6,000–$9,000 at the closing table.

 

📰 From the Blog

2026 VA Loan Changes: What Jacksonville Veterans Need to Know

The VA loan program got several major upgrades for 2026. Here are the four changes that matter most to Jacksonville veterans:

1

Higher Loan Limit: $832,750

The 2026 baseline limit jumped from $806,500. But if you have full entitlement, there's no loan limit at all — you can buy with $0 down at any price. Jacksonville's $300K median is well within reach.
2

Veterans Can Now Pay Their Own Agent Commission

Following the NAR settlement, the VA updated its policy so veterans are no longer locked out when sellers won't cover buyer-agent fees. In most Jacksonville deals, sellers still cover this — but having the option protects you in competitive situations.
3

Funding Fee Is Now Tax-Deductible

Starting in 2026, the VA funding fee (0.5%–3.3% of the loan) can be deducted on your federal return. On a $300K purchase, that's roughly $6,900 deductible. Veterans with a 10%+ disability rating are already fee-exempt.
4

VA Rates Are Beating Conventional (~5.625%)

VA borrowers are currently getting rates 0.25–0.50% below conventional buyers — with no PMI and $0 down. The monthly savings compared to a conventional loan on the same home can be $200–$400/month.
