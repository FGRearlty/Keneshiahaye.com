Big news this week: the 30-year fixed rate briefly dipped below 6% — the first time in over a year. Jacksonville's median home price is holding steady around $300,000, inventory sits at roughly 5 months of supply, and homes are averaging 47–54 days on market. That puts us squarely in balanced territory — which means buyers have real negotiating power right now, and sellers who price correctly are still closing deals.

Jacksonville was also just named one of NAR's Top Markets to Watch in 2026 — fueled by job growth, population gains, and one of the strongest price-to-income ratios in Florida. Good fundamentals for the long game.

 

Tip of the Week

Ask for Closing Cost Credits — Sellers Are Saying Yes

In today's balanced market, only 12% of Jacksonville homes are selling over asking price. That means you can negotiate. Ask your agent to request seller-paid closing cost credits or a rate buydown — in many cases, sellers would rather close the deal than drop their price. This single strategy can save buyers thousands at the table.
