If I could give every first-time buyer one piece of advice, it would be: Get pre-approved BEFORE you start looking at homes.

I know it's tempting to browse listings first, but trust me — getting pre-approved saves you time, stress, and heartbreak. Here's why:

1. Know Your Real Budget

Stop guessing what you can afford. A pre-approval tells you your exact price range so you only tour homes within reach.
 

2. Sellers Take Your Offer Seriously

In a competitive market, a pre-approval letter shows sellers you're a qualified, serious buyer — not just window shopping.
 

3. Speeds Up the Closing Process

Much of the paperwork is already done, meaning you can move faster from offer to keys in hand.

What You'll Need for Pre-Approval:

•  W-2 forms (last 2 years)

•  Federal tax returns (last 2 years)

•  Bank statements (last 2-3 months)

•  Recent pay stubs (last 30 days)

•  Photo ID (driver's license or passport)
