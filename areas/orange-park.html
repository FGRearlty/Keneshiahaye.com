About the Area

Why Orange Park?

Orange Park is a tight-knit community in Clay County known for its excellent schools, low crime rates, and genuine small-town feel — all within a short drive of Jacksonville's job centers and amenities.

The Clay County School District consistently ranks among the best in Northeast Florida, making Orange Park a top choice for families. The area offers a mix of established neighborhoods with mature trees and newer developments with modern amenities, all at price points well below Jacksonville's beach communities.

From the shops along Blanding Boulevard to the riverfront parks and the historic Orange Park town center, the community has everything you need while maintaining a relaxed pace of life. Keneshia's office is right here in Orange Park, making her uniquely positioned to serve this market.

Local Tips

Dos & Don'ts of Moving to Orange Park

Insider tips to help you make the most of your move to Clay County.

Do This

1

Research Clay County schools — consistently ranked among the best in Florida

2

Compare HOA communities — Oakleaf, Eagle Harbor, and FI Plantation each have different amenities and fees

3

Factor in the commute — Orange Park to downtown Jax is 25–40 minutes depending on traffic and route

4

Get to know the local feel — OP has a small-town vibe with big-city access, which is a major selling point

5

Explore waterfront properties — Black Creek and St. Johns River offer some hidden gem homesites

Don't Do This

1

Don't confuse Orange Park with Fleming Island — they're adjacent but have different zip codes, schools, and pricing

2

Don't skip flood insurance research — properties near Black Creek and the St. Johns River may be in flood zones

3

Don't overlook older neighborhoods — the Town of Orange Park has charming, established homes at great prices

4

Don't assume it's too far from the beach — you're only 45 minutes from Jacksonville Beach and Ponte Vedra

5

Don't forget the toll roads — some commute routes (First Coast Expressway) have tolls

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about living in and moving to Orange Park, FL.

Is Orange Park a good place to live?
Yes! Orange Park consistently ranks as one of the best suburbs of Jacksonville. It offers top-rated Clay County schools, affordable housing, low crime, and a family-friendly atmosphere with easy access to Jacksonville's amenities.
What are the best neighborhoods in Orange Park?
Oakleaf Plantation is the largest master-planned community with resort-style amenities. Fleming Island Plantation offers golf and waterfront living. The historic Town of Orange Park has charming older homes with character and walkability.
How far is Orange Park from Jacksonville?
Orange Park is about 15 miles south of downtown Jacksonville, typically a 25–40 minute commute depending on traffic. NAS Jacksonville is only 15 minutes away, making it popular with military families.
What are property taxes like in Clay County?
Clay County's millage rate is generally lower than Duval County (Jacksonville). Combined with more affordable home prices, many buyers find they get more home for their money in Orange Park compared to Jacksonville.
Are there good schools in Orange Park?
Clay County schools are consistently rated A by the Florida Department of Education. Notable schools include Fleming Island High School, Oakleaf High School, and Orange Park High School.
Is Orange Park good for military families?
Absolutely. Orange Park is just 15 minutes from NAS Jacksonville and 30 minutes from Naval Station Mayport. The area has a large military community, VA-friendly lenders, and neighborhoods accustomed to military families.
