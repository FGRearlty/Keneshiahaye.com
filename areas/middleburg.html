About the Area

Why Middleburg?

Middleburg is a thriving community in Clay County, about 25 miles southwest of Jacksonville. Known for its equestrian lifestyle, spacious properties, and family-friendly neighborhoods, Middleburg offers a unique blend of rural charm and modern convenience.

Middleburg offers affordable homes with larger lots, excellent schools through the Clay County School District, and a welcoming small-town atmosphere. Whether you're looking for horse property, a new construction home, or a peaceful place to raise a family, Middleburg has options that are hard to find closer to the city.

With easy commuting access to Jacksonville, Orange Park, and NAS Jacksonville, Middleburg is an ideal choice for military families, first-time buyers, and anyone seeking more space without sacrificing connectivity. Local Tips

Dos & Don'ts of Moving to Middleburg

Insider tips to help you make the most of your move to this Clay County gem.

Do This

1

Tour properties at different times of day — Middleburg's rural roads can feel different during rush hour vs. weekends

2

Check well and septic requirements — many Middleburg properties use well water and septic systems instead of city utilities

3

Research Clay County schools — consistently ranked among the best in Florida, with Middleburg High earning strong ratings

4

Explore the equestrian community — if you love horses, Middleburg has some of the best horse properties in Northeast Florida

5

Consider the new construction options — Branan Field area has modern communities with builder warranties and energy-efficient homes

Don't Do This

1

Don't underestimate the commute — Middleburg to downtown Jax is 35–45 minutes, so plan your route based on your workplace

2

Don't skip flood zone research — properties near Black Creek and low-lying areas may require flood insurance

3

Don't assume all properties have city water — many rural homes in Middleburg rely on wells, so get a water quality test during inspection

4

Don't overlook zoning for horse property — not all large lots are zoned for horses or livestock, so verify before buying

5

Don't forget the toll roads — some commute routes like the First Coast Expressway have tolls that add up over time

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about living in and moving to Middleburg, FL.

Is Middleburg a good place to live?
Yes! Middleburg offers a peaceful, rural lifestyle with spacious properties, affordable homes, and a strong sense of community. It's part of Clay County's excellent school district and provides easy access to Jacksonville while maintaining its small-town charm.
What are the best neighborhoods in Middleburg?
Popular areas include Branan Field with newer construction communities, Black Creek for waterfront living, Ridaught Landing for established homes, and Swimming Pen Creek for a rural lifestyle. Horse property communities are also highly sought after.
How far is Middleburg from Jacksonville?
Middleburg is about 25 miles southwest of downtown Jacksonville, typically a 35–45 minute commute depending on traffic. Orange Park and NAS Jacksonville are about 20 minutes away.
What are property taxes like in Middleburg?
Middleburg is in Clay County, which generally has a lower millage rate than Duval County (Jacksonville). Combined with more affordable home prices and larger lots, buyers often get significantly more property for their money compared to urban areas.
Are there good schools in Middleburg?
Clay County schools are consistently rated A by the Florida Department of Education. Middleburg is served by Middleburg High School, Wilkinson Junior High, and several well-regarded elementary schools.
Is Middleburg good for military families?
Absolutely. Middleburg is about 20 minutes from NAS Jacksonville and 40 minutes from Naval Station Mayport. The area has a strong military community, VA-friendly lenders, and affordable homes that work well with BAH rates.
