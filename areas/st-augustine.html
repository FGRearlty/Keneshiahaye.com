About the Area

Why St. Augustine?

St. Augustine is unlike any other city in Florida. Founded in 1565, America's oldest city offers cobblestone streets, Spanish colonial architecture, and a vibrant arts and food scene — all wrapped in the warmth of a coastal community. The real estate market here ranges from historic downtown condos to beachfront estates and modern planned communities.

St. Johns County consistently ranks as the top school district in Florida, making this area a magnet for families willing to invest in their children's education. The combination of world-class schools, low crime rates, and stunning natural beauty creates exceptional property value retention.

Whether you're drawn to the historic district's walkability, the beach communities of St. Augustine Beach and Crescent Beach, or the newer developments in the World Golf Village area, Keneshia can help you navigate this unique and competitive market.

Things to Do in St. Augustine

From historic landmarks to beautiful beaches, St. Augustine offers something for everyone.

Historic Downtown

Walk cobblestone streets on St. George Street, visit Castillo de San Marcos (built 1672), explore Flagler College's stunning Gilded Age architecture.

Beaches & Water Sports

St. Augustine Beach offers surfing, paddleboarding, and beachcombing. Vilano Beach is perfect for kiteboarding. Crescent Beach is family-friendly.

Nature & Wildlife

Anastasia State Park, Fort Matanzas, Guana Reserve, and the St. Augustine Alligator Farm. Dolphins and sea turtles are common sights.

Arts & Culture

First Friday Art Walk, Lightner Museum, St. Augustine Amphitheatre for live concerts, and dozens of galleries and craft shops.

Food & Spirits

Famous for shrimp, datil pepper sauce, and craft distilleries. Try Columbia Restaurant for Spanish cuisine or Cap's on the Water for waterfront dining.

Festivals Year-Round

Nights of Lights (Nov–Jan) is world-famous. Plus Greek Festival, Seafood Festival, Bike Week, and the St. Augustine Film Festival.

Local Insights

Dos & Don'ts of Moving to St. Augustine

Insider tips to help you make the smartest move to America's oldest city.

Do

Research flood zones carefully

Being coastal, some areas are in FEMA flood zones requiring extra insurance.

Embrace the tourist seasons

Peak tourist times (spring break, summer, holidays) bring traffic but also economic vitality.

Explore beyond the historic district

World Golf Village, Palencia, and Nocatee offer modern suburban living.

Check school zones

St. Johns County has Florida's #1 ranked school district, but enrollment boundaries matter.

Budget for coastal insurance

Windstorm and flood insurance can add to your monthly costs.

Don't

Buy on historic streets without understanding regulations

The historic district has strict renovation and building codes.

Underestimate the commute to Jacksonville

It's 40–50 minutes to downtown Jax, longer in rush hour.

Skip a thorough home inspection

Older homes may have hidden issues, and coastal properties face salt air damage.

Ignore parking challenges

The historic district and beach areas have limited parking, especially on weekends.

Miss the off-season charm

Summer heat thins out the tourists, and locals enjoy the beaches and restaurants more.

Common Questions

St. Augustine FAQ

Answers to the most common questions about living in St. Augustine.

Is St. Augustine a good place to live year-round?
Absolutely! While known as a tourist destination, St. Augustine has a thriving year-round community. Residents enjoy world-class schools, beautiful beaches, rich culture, and a walkable historic downtown. The tourist economy provides jobs and keeps restaurants and shops vibrant.

What are the best neighborhoods in St. Augustine?
It depends on your lifestyle. The historic district offers walkability and charm. St. Augustine Beach is perfect for beach lovers. World Golf Village and Palencia are family-friendly master-planned communities. Nocatee (shared with Ponte Vedra) has resort-style amenities.

How are the schools in St. Augustine?
Exceptional! St. Johns County School District is consistently ranked #1 in Florida. Notable schools include Nease High School, Bartram Trail High School, and Ponte Vedra High School. This is a major draw for families relocating to the area.

What's the real estate market like in St. Augustine?
St. Augustine's market is competitive with a median price around $385K. Historic homes can range widely in price. New construction in communities like Nocatee and Palencia offers modern amenities. The strong school district and tourism appeal drive steady home value appreciation.

How far is St. Augustine from Jacksonville?
About 40 miles south, typically a 45–50 minute drive via I-95. Many residents commute to Jacksonville for work while enjoying St. Augustine's quality of life. Some employers in the World Golf Village area reduce the commute significantly.

What about hurricanes in St. Augustine?
Like all coastal Florida areas, St. Augustine is in the hurricane zone. However, the city's location on the northeast coast means it faces fewer direct hits than South Florida. Most storms bring heavy rain and minor wind damage. Proper insurance and hurricane preparation are essential.
