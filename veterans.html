Your Benefits

VA Loan Advantages

Your military service has earned you one of the most powerful home loan benefits available. Here is what makes the VA loan unbeatable.

💰

$0 Down Payment

Purchase a home with zero money down. No need to save for years — your service qualifies you for 100% financing on eligible properties.

📈

Competitive Interest Rates

VA loans consistently offer lower interest rates than conventional mortgages, saving you thousands of dollars over the life of your loan.

💥

No PMI Required

Unlike conventional loans, VA loans never require private mortgage insurance — even with $0 down. That means lower monthly payments for you.

📋

Flexible Credit Requirements

VA loans are more forgiving on credit scores than conventional loans. Even if your credit is not perfect, homeownership may still be within reach.

💵

Limited Closing Costs

The VA limits the closing costs that lenders can charge you, and sellers can contribute toward your closing costs to save you even more.

🔄

Reusable Benefit

Your VA loan benefit is not a one-time deal. You can use it again and again throughout your lifetime, whether you PCS or upgrade your home.

VA Loan FAQs

Common VA Loan Questions

A VA home loan is a mortgage guaranteed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs. It allows eligible veterans, active-duty service members, and surviving spouses to purchase a home with zero down payment, no private mortgage insurance (PMI), and competitive interest rates.

VA loan eligibility includes veterans, active-duty service members, National Guard and Reserve members with qualifying service, and surviving spouses of veterans who died in service or from a service-connected disability. You'll need a Certificate of Eligibility (COE) which I can help you obtain.

Yes! Your VA loan benefit is reusable. If you've paid off your previous VA loan or sold the property, you can restore your full entitlement and use it again. Even with an existing VA loan, you may have remaining entitlement for a second VA loan.

The VA funding fee is a one-time payment (1.25% to 3.3% of the loan amount) that helps fund the VA loan program. It can be rolled into the loan amount. Some veterans are exempt, including those with service-connected disabilities and surviving spouses.
